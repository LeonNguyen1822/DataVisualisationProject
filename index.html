Syrian Migration Crisis

Leon Nguyen, Neel Patel

The purpose of this project is to understand the Syrian migration crisis and raise awareness of the scale and scope of the crisis and to mobilize support for those affected by the conflict. The crisis has had significant humanitarian implications, with millions of people forced to flee their homes and seek refuge in neighboring countries or beyond. The crisis has also created significant challenges for host countries and communities, with a massive influx of refugees straining resources and creating social and economic pressures

We have built two visualisations for this project. The first visualisation is a line chart, with a stacked bar chart in each cell, which visualises the trend and comparison of each year migration in Syria. The second visualisation is a choropleth map that visualises the number of people that Syrian comes to which country most.

Through the visualisations, we hope that our audience can learn interesting details about the Syrian Migration Crisis trend, better understanding the Syrian migration crisis is critical for developing effective policies and responses to support refugees and address the underlying causes of the conflict. The crisis requires a comprehensive and coordinated approach that prioritizes the needs and rights of refugees and promotes greater international cooperation and solidarity.
